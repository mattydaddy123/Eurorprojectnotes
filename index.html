Renaissance and Exploration (1450-1648)

Key Topics

  • Italian Renaissance: A period of great cultural change and achievement, marked by a revival of classical learning and wisdom.
  • Northern Renaissance: Similar to the Italian Renaissance but more focused on religious reform.
  • Age of Exploration: European explorers like Columbus and Vasco da Gama opened up new trade routes and territories.
  • Emergence of Nation-States: The development of centralized monarchies in France, Spain, and England.
  • Protestant Reformation: A movement against the practices of the Catholic Church, leading to the creation of Protestant churches.

Skills

  • Analyzing primary sources from Renaissance thinkers.
  • Comparing different explorers and their impacts.

Sample LEQ

Analyze the impact of the Renaissance on European society in the 15th and 16th centuries.

The 18th Century: European States, Wars, and Social Change (1648-1815)

Key Topics

  • Enlightened Absolutism: Monarchs like Frederick the Great and Catherine the Great who implemented Enlightenment ideas while maintaining royal powers.
  • Wars of the 18th Century: Conflicts like the War of Austrian Succession and the Seven Years' War.
  • Social Change: Shifts in the structure of European society.
  • Economic Expansion and Trade: Growth of commerce, trade, and the beginnings of the Industrial Revolution.

Skills

  • Understanding the causes and effects of 18th-century conflicts.
  • Analyzing the changes in social structures and economies during this period.

Sample LEQ

Evaluate the extent to which Enlightened Absolutism was influenced by Enlightenment ideas in the 18th century.

State-Building and the Search for Order in the 17th Century

Key Topics

  • Social Crises, War, and Rebellion: Population growth and recession, witchcraft craze, Thirty Years' War, military revolution.
  • Absolutism: Foundations in France, Richelieu, Mazarin, and the reign of Louis XIV.
  • Decline of Spain: Financial problems, ineffective leadership, and military defeats.
  • Central, Eastern, and Northern Europe: Rise of Prussia, Austria, and Russia, the Great Northern States, and the Ottoman Empire.
  • Limited Monarchy and Republics: Weakness of Polish monarchy, Dutch Republic's golden age, and England's emergence of constitutional monarchy.
  • The Flourishing of European Culture: Changes in art, theater, and the rise of new artistic styles.

Skills

  • Analyzing the causes and effects of state-building efforts.
  • Understanding the cultural changes in 17th-century Europe.

Sample LEQ

Analyze the methods and success of absolute monarchs in centralizing their states in the 17th century.

The Enlightenment (18th Century)

Key Topics

  • Philosophical Foundations: Locke, Newton, and the application of scientific reasoning to society.
  • Key Philosophes: Montesquieu, Voltaire, Diderot, Rousseau, and their contributions to political and social thought.
  • Enlightenment Ideas: Concepts of natural rights, free market economy by Adam Smith, and social contract theory.
  • Impact of the Enlightenment: Spread of Enlightenment ideas in the American and French Revolutions, contributions and limitations of Enlightenment thought.

Skills

  • Understanding the main ideas and figures of the Enlightenment.
  • Evaluating the impact of Enlightenment ideas on political and social reforms.

Sample LEQ

Evaluate the extent to which the Enlightenment promoted democratic ideals in the 18th century.

The French Revolution and Napoleon (1789-1815)

Key Topics

  • The Beginning of the Era: The American Revolution, taxation without representation, Enlightenment influences.
  • The War for Independence: Continental Congress, George Washington, internal divisions, foreign assistance, Treaty of Paris.
  • Forming a New Nation: Articles of Confederation, Constitutional Convention, federal system, branches of government.
  • Impact of the American Revolution in Europe: Enlightenment ideas, Lafayette, French Revolution influences.
  • Background to the French Revolution: Economic growth, social structure of the Old Regime, financial crises.
  • The French Revolution: Estates-General, National Assembly, peasant rebellions, abolition of feudalism, Declaration of the Rights of Man.
  • The Era of Napoleon: Rise to power, domestic policies, military campaigns, Napoleonic Code.

Skills

  • Understanding the causes and events of the French Revolution.
  • Analyzing the impact of Napoleon's rule on France and Europe.

Sample LEQ

Analyze the extent to which the French Revolution and Napoleonic Wars contributed to the spread of Enlightenment ideas.

Industrialization and Society (1815-1914)

Key Topics

  • Industrial Revolution: Transition to new manufacturing processes in Europe.
  • Social Changes: The rise of the working and middle classes, urbanization, and changes in family structures.
  • Nationalism: Movements for national unification, especially in Italy and Germany.
  • Imperialism: European powers expanding their territories overseas.
  • Unifications of Italy and Germany: Key events and figures like Garibaldi and Bismarck.

Skills

  • Assessing the economic and social impacts of industrialization.
  • Understanding the causes and consequences of imperialism.

Sample LEQ

Evaluate the extent to which the Industrial Revolution brought about changes in European social structures and family life.

Nationalism and the Unification of Italy and Germany (1815-1871)

Key Topics

  • Unification of Italy: Key events and figures like Cavour, Garibaldi, and the role of foreign powers.
  • Unification of Germany: Otto von Bismarck, the wars of unification, and the establishment of the German Empire.
  • Impact of Unification: How the unifications of Italy and Germany altered the balance of power in Europe.

Skills

  • Analyzing the causes and processes of national unification.
  • Understanding the impact of nationalism on European politics and society.

Sample LEQ

Analyze the factors that contributed to the unification of Germany under Prussian leadership.

Conflict and Tension (1914-1945)

Key Topics

  • World War I: Causes, major events, and consequences.
  • Interwar Period: Economic instability and the rise of totalitarian regimes.
  • World War II: Global conflict involving most of the world's nations.
  • Holocaust: The genocide of six million Jews by Nazi Germany.

Skills

  • Analyzing the causes and outcomes of global conflicts.
  • Understanding the economic and political instability between the wars.

Sample LEQ

Evaluate the causes of World War I and the extent to which it was inevitable.

Cold War and Contemporary Europe (1945-Present)

Key Topics

  • Cold War: The geopolitical tension between the Soviet Union and the United States.
  • European Integration: The process of European countries integrating economically and politically.
  • Fall of Communism: The collapse of communist regimes in Eastern Europe.
  • Challenges of the 21st Century: Issues such as migration and economic crises.

Skills

  • Evaluating the effects of the Cold War on Europe.
  • Understanding the process and implications of European integration.

Sample LEQ

Analyze the factors that led to the end of the Cold War and the collapse of the Soviet Union.
